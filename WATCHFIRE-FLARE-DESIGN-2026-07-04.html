Watchfire & Flare

An NPU charm for the guard dog (mechnet_watchdog) — cross-source correlation, one engine, two sensitivities  DESIGN v0.3 — 2026-07-04  SLICE 0 BUILT + LIVE — preflight + remediate  names floated, not ratified  extends mechnet_watchdog (P4)

The ask (Derek, 2026-07-04): OMEN has an idle Intel AI Boost NPU sitting at 0%. It would be a natural home for catching "empty logs and base-level failures." Turn it up when you suspect something's wrong or want in-depth live debugging; in the offtime let it quietly classify, sort and organize logs and requests. And give it a health-check correlation job: read the current workload, the HEARTH calls, AM4's physical specs and the OTel traces, take snapshots, and look for the obvious gaps. Expose it so others can quickly check — especially around new work, infra changes, and integration points. Front it with an /armdebug command: another set of eyes, on demand, just in case.
The names (from the fire-on-the-steppe cosmology — proposed, awaiting Derek's ratify): a watchfire is the standing beacon that warns the whole network when something's wrong — the idle, always-there sense. A flare is the bright light you send up when you suspect trouble, lighting the whole field for a moment. Watchfire watches; you fire a flare when you need to see.

Not a new watcher — a charm for the guard dog we already have

We already have the guard dog: mechnet_watchdog (Banked Fire P4) — it patrols on a schedule and already self-heals (revives the gateway, recovers nodes). Watchfire/Flare is not a rival watcher. Think of the watchdog as a familiar that learns spells, and the NPU as a charm we hand it. This collapses the whole design from "a new system" into "new spells + a charm on the watchdog we've got" — less to build, more coherent.
  • The charm upgrades its sense from liveness to coherence. Today the guard dog barks when something is down. But nothing was down this session — every node was "up"; the signals just disagreed. The charm teaches it to bark at incoherence, not just death: do the sources agree? That is exactly the sense that was missing.
  • The spellbook grows. Each gap-check it learns is a spell it can cast forever after — rules-spells now, NPU-learned spells later (the same rules→learned slice plan, reframed as the familiar's spellbook filling in).
  • Because it already heals, the charm expands what it can heal. Some gaps are safe to auto-cast a healing spell on — stub a confirmed-errored run and release its phantom occupancy (what we did by hand today), freshen a stale checkout. Others stay flag-only — eyes handed to a reasoner, never auto-fixed. The line is the same discipline as the assay (a gate, not an oracle) and the arm/disarm toggle: auto-heal only the boring, reversible, high-confidence spells; flag everything ambiguous. A familiar that miscasts a heal on a false positive is worse than one that just points.
/armdebug is simply "familiar, cast the flare" — raise the NPU deep-sight on command.

Born from a debugging session — the evidence

This design isn't abstract. On 2026-07-04 the HEARTH task lane was found broken against the live conductor, and every false signal that cost us time was the same shape: a gap between two sources that should have agreed, which no single view reconciled.

All three were caught by hand — grepping the conductor log, reading a result stub, decoding a builder's question — roughly 50 minutes after the first failure. A standing correlation that snapshots the planes and asks "do these agree?" catches all three at the instant they diverge.

The core insight: the fans, digitized

Derek caught the first phantom because he could hear the room — "I see them entering the board but not moving," and later the fans staying quiet when the board claimed work was running. That is a human reading mechanical truth (fan noise) against a logical claim (the dashboard) and trusting the fans.

Reading AM4's physical specs is exactly that signal, digitized. When HEARTH says "running on am4-worker-1" and the AM4 GPU reads 0% with no OTel span for four minutes — that's the board lying and the fans telling the truth, except the NPU hears the fans for you. The whole system stops needing Derek's ears in the loop.

One engine: cross-source correlation

Everything above — the failure detector, the deep debug mode, the health snapshot — is not three features. It is one primitive: pull the planes into a single frame, cross-check the logical claim against the mechanical/other-source truth, and name what disagrees.

Idle prepares the corpus that makes the armed mode fast and smart. The loop sharpens itself on your actual failures.

What the NPU actually is — the honest boundary

The Intel AI Boost NPU is a low-power, always-on accelerator for small quantized models (ONNX via OpenVINO / DirectML). It is a pattern classifier / anomaly scorer, not a reasoner — it will not root-cause a bug or understand a retro.
  • Right engine because it's the idle one. The RTX does the B70 builds; the CPU runs the conductor; the NPU sits at 0%. A standing watcher must never compete with a build — so it belongs here by elimination, not just capability. Reading AM4's specs across the network reuses the observability role + Jaeger that already carry that telemetry.
  • Microscope + triage, not detective. It gets you to the smoking log line in seconds and names the two sources that disagree; the reasoning still needs a reasoner (frontier, or Derek). Today it would have surfaced FanOutEdgeGroup must contain at least two targets the instant the run errored, ranked #1, with the eight prior identical crashes clustered beside it — collapsing the time-to-signal, not the thinking.

Rules first, NPU second — the discipline that keeps it honest

Every failure this session was rule-catchable — no ML required. Reaching for the NPU on day one would be instrumenting before there's signal, the exact anti-pattern the belief layer already punished ("trustworthy reps, not raw reps"). So the NPU is the learned upgrade, earned once the rules have generated a labeled corpus.

Where it fits — the operator toolkit

/armdebug is a repo skill in the family we already have, same pattern as /retro: default-off, authored, suspendable — the exact arm/disarm toggle Banked Fire already uses (authored, suspendable, auto-disarms so it never becomes ambient surveillance you forgot to turn off).

The recursion worth naming: today Derek was the second set of eyes — "I see them entering but not moving" was a human watching the board while the agent was buried in SSH. /armdebug hands that job to a tool so Derek isn't the only backstop next time. It's the scaling engine running on itself: he felt the need → described it → it becomes something to offload instead of something he has to be.
