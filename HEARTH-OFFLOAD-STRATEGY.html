2 · Assessment — what actually blocks the goal

The plumbing is done. Four rungs, one door, clean routing, everything ledgered. What's missing is not another backend — it's the four things that turn plumbing into an operating mode:

  1. The ferrying tax. local_generate makes Claude paste every byte of context into the prompt. For "summarize what I already read" that's fine; for "read these five files and draft X" Claude pays frontier tokens to read them first, which defeats the point. The door already has fs tools — the rungs just can't use them. This is the single biggest ergonomic lever.
  2. Verification capacity. If Claude offloads the doing, Claude's job becomes specify + verify. Verification must itself be mostly offloaded (judge panels, acceptance criteria, tests) or Claude becomes the bottleneck again. The rejudge experiments already proved the pattern and calibrated the noise (within-cell σ≈0.44 vs concise-effect≈3.0 → small panels suffice) — it just isn't packaged as a tool.
  3. The learning loop is half-open. Knowledge projections frozen at 2026-07-04 — eleven days of ledger (including the whole Gemini era) undigested. And the economics view buckets by caller, so it literally cannot see the offload ratio we're trying to maximize.
  4. Policy is prose, not mechanics. CLAUDE.md says "offload the grunt work" but nothing measures whether a session did. No KPI, no trial-credit burn-down, no per-session readout. What isn't measured won't invert.

Plus one reliability drag: AM4/oxen instability is the recurring failure across the harness (502 on the critic card, wake_am4 revives only the planner slot, 8-consecutive-failure stalls) — and the task lane survives on a workaround for a conductor bug this repo doesn't own.

5 · KPIs

  • Offload ratio: generation tokens executed on sunk/trial rungs ÷ all door generation tokens — target ≥ 80% (WP2.1 makes it computable).
  • Metered-token trend: Claude's per-session metered output for comparable work, trending down (Derek reads this from usage; /retro cross-references).
  • Knowledge freshness: projection mtime lag < 24 h (vs 11 days today).
  • Rung reliability: am4-oxen ok_rate ≥ 95% on panel duty; task-lane phantom runs = 0.
  • Trial-credit runway: burn line visible every /checkmcp; degradation runbook ready before exhaustion.

6 · Risks & contingencies

  • Trial credits end → flash starts failing mid-tag-route. Mitigation: WP2.3 burn watch + re-tag runbook; the ladder degrades gracefully to oxen/omen (sunk rungs are the floor, not the ceiling).
  • AM4 flakiness mid-panel → panels quorum-tolerant (N−1 votes accepted); WP4.1 revive fix.
  • Over-offloading judgment → the ladder's "Claude keeps" list is a hard boundary; panel votes are advisory input to Claude's decision, never binding.
  • One-boundary erosion → WP1.2 removes the last legitimate reason to bypass the door; policy: all inference through HEARTH, no raw POSTs.
  • Conductor ownership → task-lane fix stays coordination-gated; nothing in this plan writes to cc-conductor.
