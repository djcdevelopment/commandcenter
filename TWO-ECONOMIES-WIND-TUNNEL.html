Two Economies Wind Tunnel

Δ1 — Economy as Scheduling Dimension

DRAFT SUPERSEDED by Δ1′ — see ECONOMICS-ARCHITECTURE-REVIEW.html

Proposal: introduce a new scheduler decision dimension based on economic cost class (metered vs. sunk).

Initial objection: adding a third _influence field alongside policy_influence and capability_influence violates a count-based smell test.

Revised verdict: The real invariant is not the count of influence fields, but the three obligations: explain, replay, test. If a dimension satisfies those, it's constitutionally fine. The defect in Δ1 is not the count, but that cost_class: metered | sunk is a hand-assigned label with no stated derivation — a violation of Clause 1.

Redesign target: prove cost_class is either irreducible-authored (like an instrumentation decision) or derivable from usage/billing events — don't ship it as an unexamined static label.

Δ2 — Physical Telemetry Channel

DRAFT

Proposal: introduce a new physical telemetry channel for model residency.

Initial objection: model_residency: warm | cold is a classification, not a sensor reading — the warm/cold boundary is an authored threshold hiding inside what looks like telemetry.

Fix: record last_model_load_event / last_model_unload_event / model_loaded: bool as raw facts; project warm/cold downstream, same as everything else.

Δ3 — Learning Rate

DRAFT

Proposal: introduce a new learning rate metric.

Initial objection: if used to rank and select candidates, it silently becomes a decision dimension and inherits Amendment 2's obligations without anyone declaring that.

Split: keep learning-rate as a pure human-facing reporting metric, and require any ranking function that consumes it to expose its own terms the way policy_influence already does.

Δ4 — Idle-Drain Dispatch

DRAFT

Proposal: introduce an idle-drain dispatcher based on economic cost class.

Initial objection: idleness must become knowledge through events (IdleObserved / IdleEnded), never a polled wall-clock timer inside the scheduler.

Fix: use evented idle to preserve replay, determinism, and auditability.
