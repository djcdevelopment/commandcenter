Learning Rate — Critical Evaluation of Δ3

Δ3 in TWO-ECONOMIES-WIND-TUNNEL.html proposes a single scalar, knowledge per hour of runtime, as the Economy-2 objective function. This document evaluates whether that formulation survives contact with the constitution (CAPABILITY-ROADMAP.html), whether it can be gamed, and what a defensible version looks like. No code changes. No schema changes. Analysis only.

Inputs: CAPABILITY-ROADMAP.html, TWO-ECONOMIES-WIND-TUNNEL.html, contracts/*.v1.schema.json Scope: Δ3 only — Δ1/Δ2/Δ4/Δ5 referenced where they consume Δ3's output 2026-07-02
Headline verdict: Δ3 as drafted is constitutionally legal but under-specified — it is a projection in spirit, but the draft blends four incommensurable quantities into one unweighted sum, which is exactly the shape that invites Goodharting. It should ship as a metric family, not one number, and one term (belief_changed) should be replaced before it drives any dispatch decision.

Five Questions

1 — Is "learning rate" a projection?

YES, with one caveat. Every term Δ3 names — belief_before/after (experiment-result.v1), associations formed, qualifications renewed, coverage gaps closed — is already a field or state transition on a derived object. Nothing in the definition requires a human to type in a number. That satisfies Constitution Clause 1 (no organizational truth authored if it can be derived).

Caveat: the denominator, "hours of runtime," must be sourced from event-log dispatch-open/close timestamps already in events.jsonl — never from wall-clock "now." Δ3's phrasing doesn't say this explicitly. If it's computed any other way, D18 (determinism) breaks: re-projecting the same corpus at a different moment would produce a different rate for a completed, immutable run. This is a one-line fix, but it's currently an open question, not a settled one.

2 — Can it be computed entirely from existing events?

PARTIALLY — and this is the sharpest gap in the draft. Look at what each term actually requires:

TermWhat it needsStatus
belief_before/after moved A single already-materialized field pair on one experiment-result.v1 record. READY TODAY
associations formed Count of association.v1 records whose first_observed falls inside the window — fine if first_observed is trustworthy, but "formed" as a rate requires comparing the corpus at two points in time. NEEDS WINDOWING
qualifications renewed A transition: qualification_status going requalification_due → qualified. capability.v1 is a current-state snapshot, not a transition log — "renewed" is a diff between two projection runs, not a field on one record. NEEDS DIFFING
coverage gaps closed A gap present in one coverage.v1 snapshot and absent in the next — same diff-across-projections problem. NEEDS DIFFING

Three of four terms are rates of change of derived state, not derived state itself. That's a second-order derivation: a deterministic diff between two projection snapshots at two evidence watermarks. It's buildable within the constitution (the spine is replayable, so the diff is itself deterministic) — but it's machinery that doesn't exist yet. Δ3's own justification ("every term already exists as a derived object") is true for one term out of four and optimistic for the other three.

3 — Which existing derived objects contribute?

ObjectFields consumed
experiment-result.v1 belief_before, belief_after (and the confidence_score nested inside each), belief_changed

association.v1 workflows[] (minItems 2 — the no-single-workflow guard), first_observed

4 — Can the metric be gamed?

YES — five distinct vectors, all stemming from the same root cause: it's an unweighted sum of a boolean and three counts, divided by time.

  • Churn farming. belief_changed is boolean, not magnitude-weighted. Flipping an uncertain finding to known_bad on one more sample scores identically to a high-confidence capability changing qualification status. Design experiments that sit near a confidence-ladder threshold and they'll oscillate cheaply — free points, no durable knowledge.
  • Qualification treadmill. Re-running qualification_run on an already-easy, already-qualified capability right before staleness_days expires resets the clock and scores "renewed" again — forever. This out-competes touching the 10 genuinely unknown coverage gaps the corpus has already stated it's blind to, because the easy capability is cheap and reliable and the unknown gaps are neither.
  • Duration bias. Rate = numerator ÷ hours. Long experiments — exactly the ones Δ2's physical telemetry exists to enable (sustained-load thermal decay over tens of minutes) — structurally score worse per hour than ten short known_bad_retest cycles, even when the long experiment is far more valuable. The metric penalizes the experiment class its own sibling delta was built to unlock.
  • Shallow coverage closure. An unmeasured_metrics gap closes the moment the metric is reported once, regardless of what it reveals. "Gap closed" and "gap closed with a useful answer" are conflated — a junk observation that formally satisfies the schema scores the same as one that changes a decision.
  • Node starvation. Scored "per node," a node can be kept off hard problems to protect its rate, inverting the actual purpose of the competing-builders A/B (compare capability under equal difficulty, not farm throughput under selected difficulty).

5 — What behaviors would maximizing it unintentionally encourage?

  • Systematic preference for cheap, short, low-stakes experiment types over qualification_run at scale or coverage_probe into genuinely unobserved envelope regions.
  • Association formation stopping at the minimum viable abstraction (exactly 2 workflows, the schema floor) rather than pursuing deeper, harder-won generalizations — the 2-workflow minimum is the cheapest "formed" count available.
  • Avoidance of true exploration. A coverage_probe into an unobserved_combo is, by definition, the experiment most likely to land on uncertain (belief_changed=false) — the metric penalizes exactly the work Stage 8 (Coverage) exists to generate demand for.
  • If wired into Δ4's idle-drain dispatch, the scheduler would learn to prefer the candidate queue's easy tail over its valuable head — the opposite of "run it hot" as originally intended (knowledge produced, not throughput).

Alternative Formulations

A

Belief movement

RECOMMENDED — replaces belief_changed

Definition

Replace the boolean belief_changed with the magnitude |confidence_score_after − confidence_score_before|, summed per experiment. confidence_score already lives inside belief_before / belief_after on every experiment-result.v1 — no schema change.

Why it's better

Directly closes gaming vector 4a (churn farming). A confidence jump from 0→0.8 now outweighs ten threshold-hugging 0.33↔0.5 flips instead of tying with each of them.

B

Uncertainty reduction

PARTIAL — directional blind spot

Definition

confidence_score_after − confidence_score_before, signed (not absolute). Rewards only movement toward a decision-usable belief.

Weakness

Under-values corrective findings. Discovering a known_good belief was actually wrong is high-value knowledge even when it moves the corpus toward uncertain (confidence_score down). Signed uncertainty reduction scores that as a loss.

C

Information gain

Rigorous, but recursive on an unvalidated curve

Definition

Treat confidence_score as a probability and take a KL-divergence / surprisal-reduction between before/after states. The formally correct version of (A) and (B).

Weakness

confidence_score = samples/(samples+2) is a heuristic evidence curve, not a calibrated probability — no prediction_bias finding yet validates that curve against real outcomes. Computing entropy over an uncalibrated number produces a precise-looking answer to an imprecise question. (Worth its own future finding: does the evidence curve predict actual reliability?)

D

Capability expansion

Too narrow alone

Definition

New capability_ids created, qualified_resources growing, or operating_envelope widening (e.g. max_context_tokens up).

Weakness

Measures reach growing, not knowledge maintained. An org that only maximized this would neglect Stage 6 decay entirely — directly opposed to the roadmap's own framing of qualification decay as "the org's protect-capability function."

E

Experiment worth realized

RECOMMENDED — primary dispatch objective

Definition

Σ worth(candidate) for candidates where belief_changed / capability transitioned, ÷ hours. worth is already an authored field on each candidate in experiment_candidates.json at plan time (Clause 2: intent, with reason).

Why it's the strongest fit

It doesn't invent a second competing valuation scheme — it sums a scalar that's already authored, reasoned, and in the audit trail. This is the cleanest Clause-1 answer: nothing new is asserted, an existing authored judgment is aggregated. It also naturally resists duration bias (4c), because worth is set by a human who already knows a thermal-decay probe is worth more than a known-bad retest.

F

Coverage improvement

Fleet dashboard, not a per-node dispatch input

Definition

% of open coverage.v1 gaps closed fleet-wide over a window.

Weakness

Structurally uneven per node — a node can show zero improvement simply because none of the open gaps match its resource envelope, which isn't underperformance. Good as a top-line KPI, wrong as a rate a scheduler dispatches against.

G

Organizational entropy reduction

Coarse — good headline, bad control signal

Definition

Total Shannon entropy across all findings' confidence distributions, before/after a window, corpus-wide.

Weakness

Elegant as a single "is the org getting smarter" number, but not attributable back to one node or one experiment when several land in the same window — unusable as a per-dispatch objective.

Mathematical Decomposition

learning_rate(node, window) =   w1 · Σ |Δconfidence_score| over experiment-results in window [belief movement — replaces belief_changed] + w2 · count(associations.first_observed ∈ window) [needs snapshot diff] + w3 · count(capabilities: requalification_due → qualified, in window) [needs snapshot diff] + w4 · count(coverage gaps: present at window_start, absent at window_end) [needs snapshot diff] ÷ Σ (dispatch_close_ts − dispatch_open_ts) over runs in window [from events.jsonl only — never wall-clock]

As drafted, Δ3 sets w1=w2=w3=w4=1 implicitly by just adding the counts. That implicit weighting is itself an authored choice hiding inside code — see Constitutional Validation below. Two honest paths: (1) collapse to a single term whose weighting is already authored elsewhere (Formulation E, worth), or (2) keep the family but report each w-term separately rather than summing across incommensurable units (a confidence-score delta and a gap-count are not the same currency).

Constitutional Validation

ClauseVerdictReasoning
Clause 1 — no authored truth if derivable PASS* Every input is already a derived field. *Contingent on building the snapshot-diff machinery for terms 2–4 (Q2) — until then, "associations formed / qualifications renewed / gaps closed" would have to be hand-tallied, which would violate Clause 1.
Clause 2 — authored ≠ truth RISK The metric itself isn't authored as truth. But the moment it feeds Δ4's dispatch priority, it becomes an objective function — and its term weights (w1..w4 above) are an authored policy choice. If those weights live implicitly in code rather than as a traced constant, that's an unaudited authored will wearing a projection's clothes.
D18 — determinism RISK Numerator terms are fine if sourced from stored timestamps/snapshots. The runtime-hours denominator must come from event-log dispatch-open/close pairs, never from "now" — Δ3's current phrasing doesn't say this explicitly and should before anything is built.
No single-workflow generalization PASS Inherited for free — the associations term rides on association.v1's own minItems: 2 workflows guard.
No silent caps OPEN If Δ4 later thresholds candidates by learning-rate score ("below X, deprioritize"), that threshold needs the same treatment coverage gaps already get — reported with a reason, never silently dropped. Not yet specified in Δ3.

Recommendation

Ship a metric family, not one scalar.

Dispatch-time objective (what Δ4's idle-drain scheduler actually optimizes): Formulation E — experiment worth realized per hour. It reuses an already-authored field (worth on the candidate), requires no new derivation machinery, and structurally resists the duration-bias and churn-farming gaming vectors because a human already priced in that a thermal-decay probe outweighs a known-bad retest.

Report the rest — don't blend it.

Belief movement (Formulation A) as a dashboard KPI per node, replacing the boolean belief_changed Δ3 currently proposes. Coverage improvement (F) and capability expansion (D) reported as separate, labeled fleet-wide counts — never summed into the dispatch scalar, because mixing "maintain" and "expand" work in one number is precisely what produces the qualification treadmill (gaming vector 4b).

Do not build the blended Δ3 scalar as drafted.

Two prerequisites first: (1) a deterministic snapshot-diff mechanism for associations/capabilities/coverage — currently missing machinery, not an existing derived object as Δ3 claims; (2) a validated calibration of confidence_score against real outcomes (a prediction_bias finding on the curve itself) before any information-gain-flavored version (C) is trusted. Until then, Formulation E is buildable today with zero new schema and zero new gaming surface beyond what worth already carries.
